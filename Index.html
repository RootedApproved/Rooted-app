ROOTED Standard — Golf
Golf apparel is dominated by synthetic moisture-wicking performance fabric more than any other clothing category. We rate it honestly: genuine natural-fiber Tier 1 picks where they exist (sweaters, some pants and polos), and clearly-labeled synthetic compromises where the market leaves no clean option.

ROOTED Standard — Workout Apparel
Athletic wear is built almost entirely on synthetic moisture-wicking fabric — even more so than golf. Merino wool is the strongest natural-fiber alternative across most subcategories; cotton jersey and french terry cover the rest. We disclose exact fiber ratios wherever the brand publishes them, and flag it clearly when they don't.
Suits & Dress Clothes

ROOTED Standard — Suits & Dress Clothes
Wool suits, cotton dress shirts, and silk ties have been the default for over a century — natural fiber is the norm here, not the exception. The real red flags are chemical "non-iron" wrinkle-resistant finishes on cotton shirts, and the rise of mass-market polyester-blend suits and blazers as a budget alternative.
Swimwear

Honest heads up: genuine natural-fiber swimwear that you actually swim in doesn't really exist — cotton and wool don't perform wet. We focus on recycled vs. virgin synthetic as the real distinction here, and reserve natural fiber for what you wear before and after you're in the water.

ROOTED Standard — Swimwear
There's no honest Tier 1 for the parts of your swimwear that actually touch water — every functional swim fabric is synthetic. Our standard here is recycled and PFC-free-treated synthetic over virgin, undisclosed alternatives. Cover-ups worn on land, though, can and should be genuine natural fiber.

        ROOTED Standard — Cookware & Kitchen
        Cookware experiences repeated heating and direct food contact, making material choice especially important. High heat accelerates the breakdown of coatings, while inert materials — stainless steel, cast iron, carbon steel, glass, and high-quality porcelain — remain stable for decades when properly maintained. The safest kitchen is generally the simplest one.

        Why We Exist

        The standard
        has to come
        from somewhere.

        Marketing is not a standard. "Natural" means nothing. "Clean" means nothing. We built the standard that should have existed years ago.

        Most people want to live healthier. The problem is not motivation — it is information. ROOTED cuts through that noise. We research every category deeply. We publish our standards publicly. We rate products against those standards with full rationale. We never accept payment from brands.

        Standards come first. Products come second. People always.

        The Tier System

        Products sit in one of four tiers based on ROOTED's published standards. Every placement includes a written rationale. No pay-to-play. Ever.

        TIER 1Rooted Approved
        No meaningful compromises. Would confidently recommend to a family member.
        • Meets all primary ROOTED standards
        • Transparent sourcing and manufacturing
        • Third-party certification confirmed
        TIER 2Good Option
        Minor compromises — documented clearly. Still recommended for most people.
        • Meets most ROOTED standards
        • One or two minor flags — always disclosed
        TIER 3Budget / Transitional
        Acceptable when cost limits better options. Not ideal long-term.
        • Meets basic safety thresholds
        • Notable compromises — clearly disclosed
        TIER 4Not Recommended
        Fails ROOTED standards. Listed for educational comparison only.
        • Fails one or more critical standards
        • Popularity is not a standard
        The Rule

        No brand can pay to improve their tier. If a brand reformulates, we update the rating with a date stamp. Tier assignments are living documents.
